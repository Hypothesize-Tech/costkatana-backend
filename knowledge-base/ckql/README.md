# CKQL: Ask About Your Costs in Plain English

## What It Is

CKQL (Cost Katana Query Language) lets you ask cost and usage questions in natural language. Instead of building reports or filters, you type a question and get structured answers.

## Example Questions

- "What were my total AI costs last month?"
- "Show top 5 projects by cost"
- "Break down costs by model"
- "Compare this week vs last week"
- "Which models cost the most?"

## What You Get

- **Natural language** – No SQL or special syntax
- **Structured results** – Tables, totals, breakdowns
- **Insights** – Cost Katana can add notes or suggested filters
- **Workspace and time filters** – Narrow by project, workspace, or timeframe

## How to Use

- **In the app** – Use CKQL in notebooks, dashboards, or chat
- **Via API** – Send your question to the CKQL endpoint with your auth token

## Limits

- **Rate limit** – 30 queries per minute for AI-powered parsing
- **Result limit** – Up to 500 results per query (default 50)

## FAQ

**Do I need to learn a query language?**  
No. CKQL understands natural language. Just ask in plain English.

**What data can I query?**  
Your usage, telemetry, and project data—anything Cost Katana tracks for your account.

**Why are there rate limits?**  
CKQL uses AI to understand your question. Limits keep the system responsive and costs predictable.
