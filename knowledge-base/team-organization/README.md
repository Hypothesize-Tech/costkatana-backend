# Teams & Organizations

## Overview

Cost Katana supports teams and organizations so you can collaborate, share projects, and manage access. You can create workspaces, invite members, set up organizations, and use community features.

## Workspaces

- **What they are** – Workspaces group your projects (e.g., by repo, path, language)
- **Switching** – Change workspace to focus on a different project; usage and context follow
- **Metadata** – Name, path, project ID, language, framework for better analytics

## Teams & Members

- **Invite members** – Add teammates to your team or project
- **Roles** – Assign roles (e.g., admin, member) to control what people can do
- **Transfer ownership** – Move team or project ownership to another member

## Organizations

- **Hierarchy** – Organizations can have parent and child orgs
- **Use cases** – Multi-tenant setups, departments, or business units
- **Management** – Create, update, and list organizations via the dashboard or API

## Onboarding

- **First-time setup** – Guided flow to create your first project
- **Magic links** – Sign in without a password using email magic links
- **Project creation** – Quick setup for new projects with sensible defaults

## Community & Support

- **Live chat** – Get help from the Cost Katana team
- **AI assistant** – Chat with an AI that knows Cost Katana
- **Activity feed** – See recent activity and updates

## FAQ

**How do I invite someone to my team?**  
Go to Team settings, click Invite, and enter their email. They’ll get an invitation to join.

**What’s the difference between a workspace and a project?**  
A project is usually one app or product. A workspace can contain multiple projects and is used to switch context.

**Can I use Cost Katana without a team?**  
Yes. You can use it individually. Teams are optional for collaboration.
